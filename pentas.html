Pentas

Home

Pentas develop beautiful star shaped flowers that bloom all summer long. They come in a variety of bold colors such as red, white, lavender, purple or pink. In very warm regions like Texas pentas are perennials by nature. They are easy to grow and attract butterflies and hummingbirds.

When to plant:

Late spring after all danger of frost has passed.

Type of Soil:

Pentas need well draining, fertile, and slightly acidic to neutral soil, enriched with organic matter (compost). They thrive in rich, loamy, sandy, well draining soil.

Watering:

Water deeply once a week, increasing frequency during hot dry spells if the top 1-2 inches of soil are dry.

Sunshine:

They thrive in full sun, and need at least six hours of direct sunlight daily.
