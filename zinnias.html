Zinnias

Zinnias are fast-growing, heat loving annuals and are part of the Asteraceae (sunflower) family. They are native to Mexico and they are hard-working, low-maintenance flowers. They bloom in a massive variety of bright colors, shapes, and sizes. They also attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

When to plant:

Plant in the spring after all danger of frost has passed.

Type of Soil:

Well draining soil is crucial, as soggy conditions can cause root rot. Mixing organic matter or compost into the soil will promote healthy growth.

Watering:

Zinnias need consistent moisture, requiring about one inch of water per week. They thrive with deeper, less frequent watering. In high heat or containers, they should be watered every 2-3 days.

Sunshine:

They require full sun, and need at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
