Lantana

Home

Lantana are flowering plants that come in many colors, such as orange, yellow, and pink. They are a hardy plant that are a trouble free choice and can survive even in hot dry gardens. They also are great at attracting butterflies and hummingbirds. They can be grown in pots or landscape beds.

When to plant:

Early summer when all danger of frost is over and the soil has warmed up.

Type of Soil:

Lantana like rich, well-draining soil. If planting in pots, be sure to use high quality all purpose potting mix. Containers need to have adequate drainage.

Watering:

Water when the top 2 inches of soil is dry. Make sure soil is moist, but not soggy. Containers lose moisture more quickly than the ground, and so will need to be watered more frequently.

Sunshine:

Lantana need at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
