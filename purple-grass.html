Purple Fountain Grass

Purple fountain grass is a popular, fast growing ornamental grass, prized for its purple foliage and feather like plumes. In South Texas they act as perennials, however in North Texas they are more like annuals since freezes will kill it. They can reach between 3-5 feet tall.

When to plant:

Spring to early summer, after the last frost has passed.

Type of Soil:

Purple fountain grass thrives in well drained average to sandy soil. They can adapt to various soil types (such as clay), however, excellent drainage is vital to prevent root rot.

Watering:

They require consistent, moderate watering during the first few months to establish roots, usually 1-2 times weekly. Once established, these plants are highly drought tolerant. They will perform best with occasional deep watering every couple of weeks.

Sunshine:

Thrives in full sun, requiring at least six hours of direct sunlight daily. Eight plus hours a day is ideal for maximum color intensity.
