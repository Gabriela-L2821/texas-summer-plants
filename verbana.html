Verbana

Home

Verbana in Texas come in several varieties and mainly produce purple/lavender or bluish lavender flowers. They are perennials, meaning they will go dormant during the winter but will return in the spring. They are excellent for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

When to plant:

Spring (March) after the last frost or fall (late September or October).

Type of Soil:

They require well draining, nutrient poor to average soil with a slightly acidic to neutral ph (5.8 to 7.2). They thrive in sandy or sandy loam soils, and are highly drought tolerant once established.

Watering:

Water daily for the first 1-2 weeks for newly planted verbana. Once established, weekly watering is recommended. During Texas heatwaves, watering twice a week may be necessary.

Sunshine:

Verbana thrive in full, direct sun, and need at least 6-8 hours of daily sunlight to bloom. Eight to ten hours of sunshine is ideal.
