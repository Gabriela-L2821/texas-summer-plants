Texas Star Hibiscus

The Texas Star Hibiscus is a hardy perennial plant that features 3-6 inch crimson, star-shaped flowers. It blooms from late spring to early fall, and can grow from four to eight feet tall. They also attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.

When to plant:

In the spring after the last frost, allowing roots to establish before summer heat.

Type of Soil:

They thrive in consistently moist, well draining and slightly acidic soil enriched with organic matter. Rich, loamy, or sandy soil is best. Mulching is a must.

Watering:

Texas Star Hibiscus require frequent watering, often daily or twice daily for container plants. For in ground plants, water deeply and thoroughly 3-4 times per week. They are native to marshes and can handle "wet feet." It is best to water plants early in the morning.

Sunshine:

They require full sun, at least 6-8 hours daily. However, they can benefit from afternoon shade during extreme heat to prevent leaf scorch and to reduce watering needs.
